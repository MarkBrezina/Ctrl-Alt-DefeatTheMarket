### supply and demand - arbitrage
As a trader, this particular situation has occured endlessly.
I am a fish trader in the markets in town. 
By chance I've become the great friend of an older fisher living just down the beach from me.
He happened upon a pirate treasure many years ago and simply fishes for fun every day.
Because of our great friendship, he lets me have 90% of his catch every day for 1$.
I can than take his catch into town on the next day and earn 100$ for it.

This opportunity is great and in essence nets me 99% return. The other traders in the market do not have this opportunity out of lack of knowledge or out of a lack of transport to and from the older fisher's house.
These kinds of opportunities would be called arbitrage, I have access to a "market", that everyone else don't, it gives me significant beneficial value.

A similar example in the world of warcraft scene was the commodity "stranglekelp" which was an essential ingredient in various products.
By chance I stumbled upon an area abundent in stranglekelp, which was unknown to a majority of players, hence giving me a significant opportunity akin to the old fisher giving away near free fish.
Stranglekelp went for, say 1000$ per piece and were rare.
